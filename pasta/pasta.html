Spaghetti alla busara

La busara refers to a pot with a sieve insert in which scampi was previously prepared.
Today the dish is usually served with lots of tomatoes and little scampi.

Expense

40 Min.

Difficulty

Medium

Ingredients for 4 Persons

8 large raw scampi in the shell (land lobster)
1 white onion
2 Garlic cloves
½ Bund Parsley
½ Chili pepper
500g Cherry tomatoes or small, fully ripe vine tomatoes
2 THE olive oil
1 shot Cognac (optional)
Salt
Pepper
400g Spaghetti

Preparation

1 / 5

Wash the scampi and cut the shell along the belly of each one lengthwise. Remove the meat from 4 scampi, cut finely and set aside. Peel the onion and garlic and chop very finely. Wash the parsley and shake dry, pick off the leaves and chop finely. Wash the chili piece and cut it very finely with the seeds.

2 / 5

Scald the tomatoes with boiling water, let them steep briefly, rinse in cold water and peel. Halve or quarter the tomatoes depending on their size.

3 / 5

Heat the olive oil in a pan with a lid. Sauté the scampi in the shell and the empty scampi shells in it. Add the onion, garlic, chilli and parsley except for a small amount and fry. Pour in the cognac if desired and let it evaporate. Mix in the tomatoes, season with salt and pepper and simmer covered over low heat for about 10 minutes.

4 / 5

Meanwhile, bring plenty of water to the boil for the pasta and add salt. Cook the pasta al dente according to the instructions on the package. Drain in a colander.

5 / 5

Fish the scampi and the empty shells out of the pot and throw away the empty shells. Mix the raw scampi pieces with the spaghetti into the sauce and cook for another minute and heat up. Mix everything with the remaining parsley, season with salt and pepper and distribute on preheated plates. Garnish with 1 scampo each and serve immediately.
